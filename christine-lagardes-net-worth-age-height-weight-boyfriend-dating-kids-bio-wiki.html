Career

In 1981, Christine Lagarde became a member of the international law firm based in Chicago called Baker & McKenzie. Her major focus was on labor and antitrust cases. Six years down the line, she became not only a partner but also its head in Western Europe. Later on, in 1995, she became a member of the executive committee. Four years later, she because the chairperson of the company, making her the first female to hold such a seat in the firm.

She has also served as a minister in the Government of France. Some of the posts that she has held include Minister for Commerce, Minister of Agriculture and Fishing as well as the Minister of the Economy, Finance and Industry.

In 2011, she took over the position of managing director of the International Monetary Fund. After that, she became the President of the European Central Bank. As of now, she still holds the position.

Awards & Achievements

In 2014 and 2019, Christine Lagarde was the 5th and 1st Most Powerful Woman in the World, respectively, according to Forbes magazine. 2017 she was number in the List of 100 Most Influential People in Multinational Organisations courtesy of a UK-based company called Richtopia. In 2019, she received the CARE Humanitarian Award from CARE and Distinguished International Leadership Award from the Atlantic Council.

She has also received national honors among them Chevalier on 13th  July 2000, promoted Officer of Order of Légion d’honneur on 6 April 2012 as well as Commander, Order of Mérite Agricole. She has also received an honorary doctorate from KU Leuven (Belgium) and Université de Montréal.
